The WEX Sales Framework

We Sell by Telling the Customer's Story

Kurt Smith's proven 101 to 202 to 303 narrative model. Every deal is a story. Every sale is the obvious next chapter.

101
Intro

Establishing Context

The 101 stage is not a pitch — it is a listening session. The goal is to understand the customer's world so precisely that you can repeat their story back to them better than they told it. You earn the right to go deeper by demonstrating that you understand their business, their pressures, and their goals before recommending anything.

Let's keep this at 101 level: who are you serving, how do you make money, and what has to go right this year for you to win?
If I can't repeat your story back to you better than you told it, I haven't earned the right to recommend anything.
202
Tension

Surfacing the Real Problem

Most teams think they have a tooling problem. Nine times out of ten it is a story, alignment, or execution problem. The 202 stage moves from symptoms to root causes — surfacing the gaps between strategy and execution, quantifying the consequences of inaction, and building the shared urgency that makes the deal inevitable.

What's really getting in the way of hitting those goals? Not the surface friction — the thing underneath that no one is talking about directly.
If nothing changes in the next 12 to 18 months, what does that mean for your growth, your team, and your own role?
303
Vision

Rewriting the Story With WEX

The 303 is a vivid, specific picture of the customer's future — same company, same people, different playbook. WEX becomes the guide, not the hero. The customer sees themselves winning with us in the story, not just buying a service from us. The deal closes because it feels like the obvious next chapter.

Let me tell you the 303 version of your story if we work together. Same people. Different results.
Here is how your board meeting sounds 12 months from now if we do this right.
Deal
Structure

Converting Story to Structure

If the 303 resonates, the deal is just agreeing on how fast you get there and where you start. WEX frames engagements in chapters — 60 to 90 day milestones with clear success criteria — so the commercial path matches the narrative and reduces perceived risk. The close is not a hard sell. It is the logical next sentence in a story you have been writing together.

We will frame this in chapters, not an all-or-nothing saga. Chapter 1 is a 60 to 90 day engagement with clear success criteria.
The commercial structure should match the story: milestones, metrics, and ownership that track directly to the outcomes we just described.
